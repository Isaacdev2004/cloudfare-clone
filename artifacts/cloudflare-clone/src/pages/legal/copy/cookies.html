What are cookies

Cookies are small text files that are placed on your device (computer, tablet, or phone) when you visit a website. They are widely used to make websites work more efficiently, to remember your preferences, and to provide information to the website operator.

Cookies may be "session cookies" (which are deleted when you close your browser) or "persistent cookies" (which remain on your device for a defined period or until you delete them).

Cookies may be "first-party cookies" (set by the website you are visiting) or "third-party cookies" (set by a different website or service).

How to control cookies

Cookie consent banner:

When you first visit our Website, a consent banner appears at the bottom of the page. You have two choices:

— Accept: Analytics cookies will be set and we will collect anonymous usage data about your visit. You can change your mind at any time (see below).

— Decline: No analytics cookies will be set and no usage data will be collected from your visit. The consent banner will not appear again for 365 days.

If you do not click either Accept or Decline, no analytics cookies are set, no analytics data is collected, and the consent banner will appear again on your next visit.

Both choices are equally accessible. We do not use dark patterns, pre-selected checkboxes, or hidden decline options.

Changing your consent:

If you previously accepted analytics and want to withdraw your consent:

— Clear your browser cookies for www.apexlyn.com.au. This will reset the consent banner and it will appear again on your next visit.

— Alternatively, contact us at hello@apexlyn.com.au and we will record your preference.

Browser settings:

You can also control cookies through your browser settings. Most browsers allow you to:

— view what cookies are currently set;

— delete individual cookies or all cookies;

— block all cookies or block third-party cookies;

— set preferences for specific websites; and

— be notified when a cookie is being set.

Please note that blocking all cookies may affect the functionality of some website features, particularly form submissions. The HubSpot functional cookies are used to prevent duplicate form submissions and to associate your form submission with a contact record.

For more information about managing cookies in your browser, visit your browser's help pages or www.allaboutcookies.org.

Opt-out of HubSpot cookies:

If you want to prevent HubSpot from setting cookies, you can block cookies from the domain hubspot.com in your browser settings. This will not prevent you from submitting forms on our Website, but it may result in duplicate form submissions not being detected.
